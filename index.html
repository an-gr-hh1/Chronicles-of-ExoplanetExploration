Different Exoplanets

Welcome!, lets start!

01. Kepler-16b

Kepler-16b orbits two stars, making it a circumbinary planet — something like a real-world "Tatooine" from Star Wars.

ORBIT PERIOD: 229 Days
Distance from Earth: ~200 light-years

02. Proxima Centauri b

Proxima Centauri b is the closest known exoplanet to Earth and orbits within its star’s habitable zone.

ORBIT PERIOD: 11.2 days
Distance from Earth: 4.24 light-years

03. TRAPPIST-1e

TRAPPIST-1e is one of seven Earth-sized planets in the TRAPPIST-1 system and is considered one of the most promising for the potential for life.

ORBIT PERIOD: 6.1 day
Distance from Earth: ~40 light-years

04. K2-18b

K2-18b is one of the few exoplanets where water vapor has been detected in its atmosphere, making it a promising candidate in the search for habitable worlds.

ORBIT PERIOD: 33 days
Distance from Earth: ~124 light-years

05. HD 189733 b

HD 189733 b is known for its deep blue color due to high-speed winds and silicate particles in its atmosphere — making it a "glass rain" planet.

ORBIT PERIOD: 2.2 days
Distance from Earth: ~64.5 light-years

06. Gliese 581g

Gliese 581g is an exoplanet that sparked debate regarding its existence and potential habitability, as it may lie within the habitable zone.

ORBIT PERIOD: 37 days
Distance from Earth: ~20 light-years

07. 55 Cancri e

55 Cancri e is believed to be a "diamond planet" due to its carbon-rich composition.

ORBIT PERIOD: 0.7 days
Distance from Earth: ~41 light-years

08. WASP-12b

WASP-12b is a "hot Jupiter," and its extreme proximity to its star leads to its atmosphere being pulled apart, making it one of the darkest exoplanets.

ORBIT PERIOD: 1.1 days
Distance from Earth: ~1,410 light-years.

09. TrES-2b

TrES-2b is the darkest known exoplanet, reflecting less than 1% of the light that hits it.

ORBIT PERIOD: 2.5 days
Distance from Earth: ~750 light-years.
